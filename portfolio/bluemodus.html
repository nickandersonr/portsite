BlueModus

Complete company rebrand

Every web agency I've worked for has wanted a better site. I've always wanted to make one, but client work always comes first - so the "Cobbler's Son" phenomenon continues for years and years, until finally, one lucky designer gets to rebuild the whole thing from scratch.

For BlueModus, that lucky designer was me. I was given full creative control to rebrand, redesign, and rebuild the entire brand guidelines, and I couldn't have been more excited.

From a brand perspective, I kept the same basic logo structure, but dropped the "modus" symbol that added needless x-length to the logo while providing no demonstrable brand recognition. I softened the colors to allow it to mingle with more color schemes, and allow our branding colors to be used in marketing decks and presentation documents.

For the design of the site, I stripped out as much clutter as possible and used opaque images and content boxes to give a soft, frosted-glass feel. While a lot of our competitors went for loud, vibrant designs with maximum information and minimum hierarchy, I felt a quieter design would be more approachable to the business-minded, CEO-types we were trying to attract.

I used Bootstrap and standard CSS for the whole site - no additional libraries. I wanted to the site to be small and simple, and if we were going to sacrifice load times anywhere, I wanted it to be on the large, high quality imagery.

At the moment this site is still in development, so all I can say is that internal feedback has been positive. I'm more than happy to show the work in progress in person, so just ask!
