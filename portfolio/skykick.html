SkyKick

Site Build

Skykick manages cloud-based migration and backup for Office365 business tools - a very specific niche that is unlikely to generate any interest from anyone not directly involved in Microsoft's business products. And that's the problem - with a business model admittedly as dorky as they come, how do you generate interest?

People who are shopping for business tool management solutions are not the same crowd mindlessly browsing sneakers on or purses online - Skykick told us that most of their inquiries are from panicked IT managers, desperately seeking a swift solution to a major business overhaul. They're probably not going to notice the 500 milliseconds of ease-in on the buttons - they're looking for clear, easy to understand information, as fast as they can take it in.

This is the core of Skykick's design - its modern and well designed, but simple - the text and callouts are clear, the sitemap is small and focused. As I worked with their designer, I helped trim away areas of congestion and get the most out of the responsive options at hand. I paid special attention to removing unneeded weight from the front-end implementation, making sure to leave behind something that the (extremely technically skilled) client could continue to tweak and build off of.

The end result is sleek and simple - while not the most unique project in my portfolio, it stands out for its pure utility and form; like a Honda Civic, or a KitchenAid Mixer. It does exactly what it sets out to do, brilliantly and efficiently.
