Kids with ADHD, like Madison, have symptoms that can change
depending on the situation within a given setting.

Evaluate symptoms across
a variety of settings

Consider the following questions:

  • Which symptoms have you seen in this
    child that concern you?
  • Do you notice certain situations
    throughout the day that provoke
    symptoms?
  • Do you notice a time of the day when
    symptoms are more pronounced?

Diagnosis should be based on a complete history and
evaluation of the patient.

Reference: American Psychiatric Association.
Attention-deficit/hyperactivity disorder. In: Diagnostic and
Statistical Manual of Mental Disorders, Fifth Edition (DSM-5).
Arlington, VA: American Psychiatric Association; 2013:59-65.
