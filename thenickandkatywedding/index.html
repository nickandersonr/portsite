The room was dark.

So dark I could only see her blue eyes because of the way the candle on the table caught them, like two windows lit up on a dark street. I searched them like a voyeur, trying to catch glimpses of whoever lived inside.

Her hands were like exposed wire, sparking as I brushed mine against hers. I balanced my wrist on her knee, tracing the lines in her palm, looking for a clue - trying to find myself on its miniature canvas.

Her face was warmer than Texas, and maybe that’s why I leaned in - it reminded me of home.
